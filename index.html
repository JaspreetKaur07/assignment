Welcome to the World of KitKat

Discover KitKat

KitKat is a chocolate-covered wafer bar that has become one of the most recognizable confections around the globe. Introduced in the United Kingdom in 1935 as “Rowntree’s Chocolate Crisp,” the KitKat brand has undergone several changes over the years but has consistently delivered the same delightful taste that chocolate lovers have come to adore.

The History of KitKat

The KitKat brand’s origins can be traced back to 1911 when Rowntree’s, a confectionery company based in York, England, trademarked the terms Kit Cat and Kit Kat. The name was inspired by the Kit-Cat Club, an 18th-century English club, and was first used for a brand of boxed chocolates in the 1920s. The iconic four-finger bar, known today as KitKat, was launched in 1935 as Rowntree’s Chocolate Crisp and was renamed KitKat Chocolate Crisp two years later. After World War II, the name was shortened to just KitKat.

Fun Facts About KitKat

  • KitKat has been around since 1935 and was originally known as "Rowntree’s Chocolate Crisp".
  • The name KitKat comes from the Kit-Cat Club, an 18th-century English club.
  • During World War II, KitKat had blue packaging due to a shortage of milk, which forced the switch from milk chocolate to dark chocolate.
  • The slogan "Have a break, have a KitKat" was first used in 1957 and has become one of the most famous advertising slogans.
  • KitKat bars were Rowntree’s best-selling product within two years of their launch.
  • In Japan, KitKat is associated with good luck and is often given as a gift to students before exams.
